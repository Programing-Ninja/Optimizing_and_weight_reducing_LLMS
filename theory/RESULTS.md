## Part B — what the toy confirmed / refined
1. **A.1 "loss is locally quadratic in ΔW" — CONFIRMED as an equality.**
   Per-layer median |cross|/quadratic ≈ 1e-4 (single-layer truncation), and the
   readout known-H check `quad == tr(ΔW G_h ΔWᵀ)` holds to
   6.9e-08. Standalone Eckart–Young control:
   ΔL == Σ_{i>r}σ_i² to 1.2e-03 (MC noise).
   **Refinement:** ΔL is *concave* in (1−η) — the last-discarded small-σ modes
   carry disproportionate loss-curvature, so α is a LOCAL slope (global
   through-origin R²=0.37 vs local R²=0.98).

2. **A.2 "TurboQuant = unbiased variance ~2^(−2b)" — CONFIRMED, with a
   finite-rate caveat.** Estimator bias²/variance ~1e-5 (effectively unbiased);
   variance and MSE distortion both decay geometrically but with **effective
   exponent p≈1.83, not the asymptotic 2** —
   the codebook's own finite-rate Lloyd–Max distortion has the same slope.

3. **A.3 additivity — MOSTLY additive, with sub-additive coupling at aggressive
   joint compression.** median |cross|/measured = 0.052,
   max = 0.460. Verdict: ADDITIVE in the operating regime (median 5.2%), but COUPLED at aggressive joint compression (up to 46.0%); coupling is sub-additive (errors partially cancel).
   The coupling is **negative**: compressing weights first lowers the KV cache
   rank, so quantization noise costs *less* loss than the additive model predicts
   — a beneficial interaction for the joint budget.

4. **Recovery-LoRA — CONFIRMED α-shrink.** Tiny adapters (rank 4)
   recover 80–97% of the SCT bias, shrinking α by **5.4×**.

## Part A — predicted joint optimum
- At 35% of the dense byte budget: **η\*=0.7080, b\*=3.579**.
- With recovery-LoRA (α 7.826e-04→1.456e-04, 5.4× smaller): η* 0.7080→0.6403 (Δ=-0.0677), b* 3.58→4.61. Lower η* ⇒ compress weights HARDER and reallocate freed bytes to KV.

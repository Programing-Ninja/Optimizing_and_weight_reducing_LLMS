## Part B — what the toy confirmed / refined
1. **A.1 "loss is locally quadratic in ΔW" — CONFIRMED as an equality.**
   Per-layer median |cross|/quadratic ≈ 1e-4 (single-layer truncation), and the
   readout known-H check `quad == tr(ΔW G_h ΔWᵀ)` holds to
   0.0e+00. Standalone Eckart–Young control:
   ΔL == Σ_{i>r}σ_i² to 1.2e-03 (MC noise).
   **But the scalar model α(1−η) is WRONG globally** — ΔL is *concave* in (1−η)
   (small-σ modes carry disproportionate loss-curvature): linear global
   R²=0.23 (iso), R²=-2.63
   (aniso). The honest summary is a power law `(1−η)^0.57`; the
   exact model is the quadratic. **The Part A solver therefore consumes the
   MEASURED distortion–rate curve, not α(1−η).**
   *Also:* truncating all layers at once is **sub-additive across layers** —
   measured ΔL is 0.23–0.98× the
   sum of per-layer quadratics (layer perturbations partially cancel).

2. **A.2 "TurboQuant = unbiased variance ~2^(−2b)" — CONFIRMED, with a
   finite-rate caveat.** Estimator bias²/variance ~1e-5 (the error distribution
   is wide but centred on 0 — see `tq_arm.png` panel 2); variance and MSE
   distortion decay geometrically but with **effective exponent
   p≈1.83, not the asymptotic 2** — the
   codebook's own finite-rate Lloyd–Max distortion has the same slope.

3. **A.3 additivity — MOSTLY additive, sub-additive coupling at aggressive
   joint compression.** median |cross|/measured = 0.052,
   max = 0.460. Verdict: ADDITIVE in the operating regime (median 5.2%), but COUPLED at aggressive joint compression (up to 46.0%); coupling is sub-additive (errors partially cancel).
   Coupling is **negative**: compressing weights first lowers the KV cache rank,
   so quantization noise costs *less* loss than additive predicts — beneficial.

4. **Recovery-LoRA — CONFIRMED α-shrink, same concave shape.** Tiny adapters
   (rank 4) recover a roughly constant **84%**
   of the SCT bias across η (so they scale the concave curve down, not flatten
   it: post-LoRA power exponent 1.15 vs
   0.74 before), shrinking the local α by
   **5.4×**.

## Part A — predicted joint optimum (measured SCT curve, balanced byte regime)
- At 50% of the dense byte budget: **η\*=0.8385,
  b\*=5.351** — an INTERIOR optimum where the KKT
  equal-marginal-loss-per-byte condition holds (marginals match to 3 sig figs).
- With recovery-LoRA (α 7.826e-04→1.456e-04, 5.4× smaller): η* 0.8385→0.3000 (Δ=-0.5385), b* 5.35→8.00. Lower η* ⇒ compress weights HARDER and reallocate freed bytes to KV.
- **Regime finding (contradicts §A.3's guess):** because measured SCT distortion
  is concave (cheap per byte), tight budgets lean on **weight compression first**,
  not "TQ first" — see the trace in `theory/models/results/regimes.png`.
